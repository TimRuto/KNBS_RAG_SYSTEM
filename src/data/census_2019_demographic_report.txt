KENYA CENSUS 2019 - DEMOGRAPHIC REPORT
Published by: Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS)
Census Date: August 24-31, 2019
Report Published: April 2020

OVERVIEW
The 2019 Population and Housing Census was the fourth national census conducted in Kenya since independence. It provided critical demographic information used for national planning, resource allocation, and policy formulation. The census employed modern ICT tools including tablets for data collection.

KEY DEMOGRAPHIC FINDINGS
1. Kenya has a youthful population with 34.1% below age 14
2. Rapid urbanization with 34.2% of population in urban areas
3. Significant rural-urban disparities in service access and poverty
4. High fertility rates, particularly in rural areas
5. Improving literacy rates with 87.3% adult literacy
6. Agriculture remains dominant livelihood for 43.9% of employed population
7. Significant internal migration driven by economic opportunities

IMPLICATIONS FOR DEVELOPMENT
- Need for expanded educational infrastructure and quality improvement
- Healthcare system must address both rural and urban needs
- Agricultural modernization crucial for rural livelihoods
- Infrastructure investment needed to support urbanization
- Employment creation essential to address 8.1% unemployment
