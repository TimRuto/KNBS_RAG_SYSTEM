KENYA INFLATION AND PRICE TRENDS 2020-2024
Published by: Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS)
Report Date: May 2024
Data Period: January 2020 - May 2024

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This report presents comprehensive analysis of inflation trends and price movements in Kenya over the 2020-2024 period. The period witnessed significant macroeconomic challenges including the COVID-19 pandemic, supply chain disruptions, currency depreciation, and monetary policy adjustments. Monthly Consumer Price Index (CPI) data and sectoral analysis are provided below.

EXCHANGE RATE IMPACT ON INFLATION
The Kenyan Shilling weakened significantly during 2020-2024:

2020: 1 USD = KES 105-110 (average)
2021: 1 USD = KES 108-112 (average)
2022: 1 USD = KES 115-125 (peak depreciation)
2023: 1 USD = KES 125-130 (stabilizing)
May 2024: 1 USD = KES 128.50

Impact:
- Imported goods prices increased by 23% from 2020-2024
- Oil prices (imported) contributed to inflation spikes
- Agricultural exports became more valuable (positive for farmers)
- Import-dependent manufacturing faced cost pressures

MONETARY POLICY RESPONSES
Central Bank Rate Adjustments:

2020: 7.0% → 7.5% (pandemic uncertainty)
2021: 7.5% (held steady)
2022: 7.5% → 8.5% → 9.75% (aggressive tightening)
2023: 9.75% → 10.5% (further tightening)
2024: 10.5% → 10.0% (May 2024 - first cut)

Interest Rate Impact on Consumer Prices:
- Higher rates increase borrowing costs
- Reduces aggregate demand and inflation
- Negative impact on investment and employment
- Delayed effects on inflation (6-12 month lag)

PRICE CONTROLS AND GOVERNMENT INTERVENTIONS
2020-2023: Various price stabilization measures
- Fertilizer subsidies reducing input costs
- Strategic grain reserves used to stabilize food prices
- Petroleum subsidy initially applied then removed
- Utility rate reviews with social protection measures
- School feeding programs (reducing demand pressure)

CONSUMER EXPENDITURE PATTERNS CHANGES

2020 (Pandemic Impact):
- Food purchases: +8% (hoarding, increased home consumption)
- Transport: -25% (lockdowns)
- Restaurants: -45% (closures)
- Online retail: +120%

2024 (Post-pandemic):
- Food purchases: Returned to baseline (with 6.2% inflation)
- Transport: Increased by 8% above pre-pandemic
- Restaurants: Exceeded pre-pandemic levels by 12%
- Online retail: 15% of total retail (steady)

OUTLOOK AND PROJECTIONS

2024 Full Year Forecast:
- Core inflation (excluding food): 3.5-4.0%
- Food inflation: 4.5-6.5% (dependent on harvest)
- Overall inflation: 4.0-5.0%
- Key Risk: Weather patterns affecting food production

2025 Projections:
- Expected moderation to 3.5-4.5% (if drought ends)
- Monetary policy normalization possible
- Exchange rate stabilization expected

CHALLENGES AND FACTORS

Inflationary Pressures Remaining:
1. Food price volatility from weather
2. Oil price global movements
3. Currency depreciation risks
4. Supply chain disruptions (post-pandemic)
5. Increased utility tariffs

Deflationary Factors:
1. Improved agricultural supplies
2. Enhanced monetary policy credibility
3. Exchange rate stability efforts
4. Reduced global inflation
5. Increased competition in services

RECOMMENDATIONS
1. Maintain disciplined monetary policy
2. Develop agricultural storage facilities (reduce seasonal price spikes)
3. Improve supply chain efficiency
4. Support exchange rate stability
5. Develop early warning systems for food security
6. Strengthen producer organizations to reduce post-harvest losses
