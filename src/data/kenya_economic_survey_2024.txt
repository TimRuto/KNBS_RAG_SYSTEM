KENYA ECONOMIC SURVEY 2024
Published by: Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS)
Publication Date: June 2024
Data Period: January 2024 - May 2024

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The Kenyan economy showed mixed performance in the first half of 2024. Overall GDP growth is estimated at 4.8% in Q1 2024, slightly below the 5.2% recorded in Q4 2023. Key sectors including agriculture, services, and manufacturing contributed to this growth, though with varying degrees of performance.

MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
- Inflation Rate: 4.6% (May 2024), down from 5.2% (March 2024)
- Exchange Rate: 1 USD = KES 128.50 (Average for Q1 2024)
- Money Supply (M3): KES 5,847 billion (March 2024)
- Foreign Exchange Reserves: USD 7.2 billion (May 2024)

SECTORAL PERFORMANCE

1. AGRICULTURE SECTOR
Agriculture contributed 32.8% to overall GDP in Q1 2024. Key highlights:
- Cereals Production: Total production of 2.1 million metric tonnes, up from 1.9 million in 2023
- Maize Production: 1.2 million metric tonnes (12% increase year-on-year)
- Coffee Production: 48,500 metric tonnes (8% increase)
- Tea Production: 67,200 metric tonnes (2% decrease due to drought in some regions)
- Horticulture Exports: USD 89.5 million (Q1 2024)

Regional Performance:
- Western Region: 156,000 hectares under cereals production
- Rift Valley: 245,000 hectares under cereals production
- Coastal Region: Significant decline in coconut production (-8.2%)

2. MANUFACTURING SECTOR
Manufacturing represented 9.2% of GDP in Q1 2024.
- Manufacturing Output Index: 102.3 points (May 2024), up from 98.7 (March 2024)
- Industrial Production: 2.1% growth year-on-year
- Key Industries: Food and beverages (38% of output), textiles (12%), chemicals (15%), metals (8%)
- Capacity Utilization Rate: 67.3% (Q1 2024)

3. SERVICES SECTOR
Services accounted for 49.2% of GDP in Q1 2024.
- Telecommunications Revenue: KES 98.3 billion (Q1 2024)
- Tourism Earnings: USD 347.2 million (Q1 2024), 18% increase from Q1 2023
- Financial Services: KES 124.6 billion in total bank lending (May 2024)
- Retail Trade Index: 105.2 points (May 2024)

EMPLOYMENT STATISTICS
Total Labor Force: 21.3 million (Q1 2024 survey)
- Employed: 18.7 million (87.8% employment rate)
- Unemployed: 2.6 million (12.2% unemployment rate)

Employment by Sector (Q1 2024):
- Agriculture, Forestry, Fishing: 6.2 million persons (33.2%)
- Manufacturing: 1.8 million persons (9.6%)
- Services: 10.1 million persons (54.1%)
- Other: 0.6 million persons (3.2%)

Regional Unemployment Rates:
- Nairobi County: 8.5%
- Mombasa County: 11.2%
- Nakuru County: 9.8%
- Kisumu County: 13.4%
- Nairobi Metropolitan Area: 7.9%

CONSUMER PRICE INDEX (CPI)
Base Year: 2016 = 100
- All Items Index: 138.2 (May 2024)
- Food and Non-Alcoholic Beverages: 149.6 (May 2024)
- Clothing and Footwear: 127.3 (May 2024)
- Housing, Water, Electricity: 132.1 (May 2024)

Year-on-Year Inflation Rates (May 2024):
- Overall Inflation: 4.6%
- Food Inflation: 6.2%
- Core Inflation (excluding food and energy): 3.8%

FOREIGN TRADE
- Total Exports (Q1 2024): USD 1.8 billion
- Total Imports (Q1 2024): USD 2.6 billion
- Trade Deficit: USD 0.8 billion

Top Export Commodities:
- Tea: USD 285 million (15.8% of total exports)
- Coffee: USD 156 million (8.6%)
- Cut Flowers: USD 198 million (11.0%)
- Fish and Fish Products: USD 143 million (7.9%)

Major Trading Partners:
- Uganda: 12.2% of total exports
- United States: 8.9% of exports
- United Kingdom: 7.3% of exports
- Pakistan: 6.1% of exports
- European Union (combined): 22.4% of exports

GOVERNMENT REVENUE AND EXPENDITURE (Jan - May 2024)
- Total Revenue Collected: KES 687.3 billion
- Tax Revenue: KES 589.2 billion
- Non-Tax Revenue: KES 98.1 billion
- Total Expenditure: KES 759.1 billion
- Budget Deficit: KES 71.8 billion

OUTLOOK AND CHALLENGES
The economy faces several headwinds including:
1. Climate variability affecting agricultural productivity
2. Global oil price volatility impacting energy costs
3. High interest rates affecting credit availability for businesses
4. Currency depreciation pressures
5. Inflationary pressures from food and energy sectors

However, positive factors include:
- Improved inflation trajectory
- Stable forex reserves
- Growing tourism sector
- Strong services sector performance
- Infrastructure development ongoing

RECOMMENDATIONS
1. Continue fiscal consolidation to manage budget deficit
2. Maintain supportive monetary policy stance
3. Strengthen agricultural productivity through modernization
4. Enhance manufacturing competitiveness
5. Support tourism sector growth through marketing and infrastructure
