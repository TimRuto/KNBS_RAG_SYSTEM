KENYA AGRICULTURAL SECTOR REPORT 2024
Published by: Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS)
Publication Date: March 2024
Data Period: 2023-2024

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The agricultural sector remains a cornerstone of the Kenyan economy, contributing approximately 32-35% to GDP and employing over 40% of the labor force. This report provides comprehensive analysis of agricultural production, productivity trends, and challenges facing the sector in 2023-2024.

SECTOR OVERVIEW
Total Agricultural Land: 28.3 million hectares (48% of total land area)
- Arable Land: 9.2 million hectares
- Permanent Crops: 3.1 million hectares
- Permanent Meadows and Pastures: 16.0 million hectares

Agricultural Workforce: 9.2 million persons
- Full-time Farmers: 5.8 million
- Part-time/Casual: 3.4 million
- Gender Distribution: Male 52%, Female 48%

AGRICULTURAL CHALLENGES

Climate Variability:
- Prolonged Droughts: Affecting arid and semi-arid regions
- Erratic Rainfall: Impacting crop planning and productivity
- Flood Events: Causing crop and livestock losses in some regions
- Estimated Climate Impact: 5-12% productivity loss annually

Production Constraints:
- Limited Access to Improved Seeds: 42% of farmers use traditional seeds
- Low Fertilizer Use: Average application 25 kg/ha (Target: 75 kg/ha)
- Poor Extension Services: Limited farmer access to advisory services
- Land Fragmentation: Average farm size 0.5 hectares (declining)
- High Input Costs: Fertilizer prices increased 35% from 2022-2023

Market Challenges:
- Low Producer Prices: Often below cost of production
- Poor Market Access: 65% of smallholder farmers lack direct market linkage
- Post-Harvest Losses: 20-30% of total production lost
- Limited Value Addition: Only 15% of agricultural output processed

RECOMMENDATIONS
1. Strengthen irrigation infrastructure and water conservation
2. Promote climate-smart agriculture practices
3. Improve seed quality and accessibility

8. Enhance market linkages for smallholder farmers
